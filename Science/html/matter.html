Get Started

Presentation:

Changes in physical properties are called physical changes.
If a substance remains the same, then the following are physical changes:

  • Size
  • Shape
  • State
If you add red food coloring to salt, what would happen?
You would still have salt. The change from white to red would be considered a physical change.
Despite the red color, it would still taste like salt!

If you crushed the salt crystals into powder, the change would be physical because you would still have salt.
In both examples, only the appearance of the salt changes, not the chemical makeup.

If you mixed the salt into a liquid until it dissolved, is that an example of a physical change?

Materials:

1/2 cup of water
Tablespoon of salt
Spoon
Cooking Pot
Bunsen Burner

STEP 1

Instructions

Dump salt and water into the pot.

Mix with the spoon until you can no longer see the solid salt.

STEP 2

Hypothesize

What happened to the salt?

Did it disappear?

How can you tell?

STEP 3

Instructions

Put the pot onto the bunsen burner and turn it on.

Heat up the salt/water mixture until boiling.

Cook until all water has evaporated, then turn off bunsen burner.

Allow the pot to cool before moving to the next step.

STEP 4

Instructions

Collect all left over salt.

Measure how much salt is remaining.

Conclusion

Analysis

How does the new amount of salt differ from the original amount?

Why do you think it changed back to salt?

Was your hypothesis correct?
